Execution

Execution focused on aligning the advertising engine with operational reality not cosmetic creative changes or surface-level optimization.

We categorized the entire catalog by: • Contribution margin • Net profitability • Restock cycles • Customer retention potential • Shipping cost impact This allowed the brand to know exactly which SKUs deserved paid promotion and which should never receive traffic. Inventory-Aware Campaign Structuring Campaigns were reorganized into tiers: • High-margin, stable-stock SKUs (priority spend) • Medium-margin SKUs (controlled testing) • Low-margin SKUs (restricted or paused) • OOS or unstable-stock items (no paid traffic) Paid spend finally aligned with operational constraints. Real-Time Stock Alerts We built automated signals that notified the marketing team when: • Inventory was running low • SKUs approached depletion • Replenishment was delayed • High-velocity items needed campaign throttling This eliminated wasted spend and prevented customer disappointment. Profit-Driven Budget Allocation Budgets were redistributed based on margin contribution, with scaling rules tied to profitability thresholds—not ROAS. This shifted spend toward the SKUs that actually grew the business. Cross-Team Alignment Marketing, operations, and finance now worked from: • The same margin data • The same inventory feed • The same SKU priority list • The same profitability model This removed friction and established a unified decision-making system.
